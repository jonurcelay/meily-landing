From Santa Cruz to SF in One Day: My Honest First Impressions

5:30 AM wake-up, Caltrain adventures, In-N-Out in a Waymo, and unbeatable Golden Gate views.

The Long Journey North

As a European visiting student at UC Santa Cruz, I decided to do a full day trip to San Francisco on Saturday, March 7th, 2026. I woke up at 5:30 AM and took the Highway 17 bus to San Jose Diridon, then hopped on the Caltrain all the way to SF. Total travel time: about 3 hours (1h bus + 1.5h train + waiting). I arrived around 9:15 AM surprisingly fresh — sleeping on the bus and train helped a lot!

My original plan was way too ambitious, covering the four colored areas and all the stops as shown in the images below.

My overly-optimistic original plan 😅

I finally focused on the light-blue and light-green zones, as it was impossible to do everything as in the initial plan. From these two zones these were the ones I actually ended up visiting:

My Final San Francisco One-Day Itinerary

1. Ferry Building & Piers Walk

My first real touch of SF: a beautiful waterfront path from the station straight to the Ferry Building. Walking along the piers felt magical — classic San Francisco vibes right away.

2. Lombard Street

The famous crooked street was a must, but honestly... it's just a street. A few photos, done. The uphill walk was brutal — most tourists wisely take the cable car (smarter and more fun!).

3. Cable Car Museum (Quick Stop)

Met a friend living in SF here. Free entry, super cool to see the giant motors pulling the cables. Only 10 minutes, but worth it.

4. Chinatown & Downtown Wandering

Nothing mind-blowing, just classic big-city energy. Got a bit lost (autopilot mode with my friend guiding) — fun to soak in the atmosphere.

5. Palace of Fine Arts + In-N-Out

Took a Waymo (robotaxi!) from downtown — my first time! It was insanely smooth and I adapted in seconds. Ate at In-N-Out (full American experience) — even got stickers and a paper hat. The waitress asked "for the kids?" and I said "hell no, for me!" 😂 The area around Palace of Fine Arts has a gorgeous walk by the water.

6. Baker Beach (Golden Gate View)

Another robotaxi to Baker Beach — sunny, hot day, beach packed. Great view of the Golden Gate, but I wanted more...

7. Golden Gate Bridge Welcome Center

Final stop: buses to the Welcome Center. Much better view than Baker Beach! Wandered around until ~18:00, then headed back. Buses were slow in traffic — robotaxis are definitely faster.

The Way Back & What I Learned

Took almost 2 hours to get back to the train station due to downtown traffic. Caltrain at 19:55 to San Jose, then Highway 17 bus — home in Santa Cruz around 22:30. Long day (little sleep, lots of walking), but totally worth it. Sunny weather helped!

Key lessons: SF is NOT flat — endless hills! Prepare your legs or use cable cars/buses/Waymos.

Top 3 experiences: (1) Golden Gate Bridge views, (2) Riding a Waymo/robotaxi (mind-blowing tech — normalized in SF!), (3) Walking the piers area.

Final Thoughts

A tiring but incredibly fun first visit to SF. What do you think — did I miss any must-see spots? Let me know in the comments if I should go back and what to add next time!
